Case Brief Summary

Case: Students for Fair Admissions, Inc. v. President & Fellows of Harvard College, 600 U.S. 181 (2023)

Facts: Students for Fair Admissions challenged Harvard and UNC’s admissions processes, arguing that the use of race as a factor in admissions decisions was discriminatory.

Issue: Does the use of race in college admissions violate the Equal Protection Clause and Title VI?

Holding: Yes, the use of race in the admissions programs violates the Equal Protection Clause and Title VI.

Reasoning: The Court applied strict scrutiny and found that the admissions programs were not narrowly tailored. The Court determined that the policies relied on racial classifications without clear limits and did not meet constitutional requirements.

Disposition: The Court reversed the lower court decisions.
